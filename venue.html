The W3C/OGC Joint Workshop on Maps for the Web is scheduled for 15−17 June, 2020, at the CAE Montreal training centre.

The workshop will be co-located with the OGC's technical and planning committee meetings. Workshop sessions will include videoconferencing and online chat, to support remote participation.

The programming committee is aware that travel restrictions related to the 2020 coronavirus outbreak may prevent many people from joining us in Montreal. We ask all participants to keep their registration status up-to-date, so that we know how many participants will be joining in person and how many remotely.

If too few people can come in person, or if the spread of the virus results in public health agencies recommending against travel to Montreal, it is possible that the in-person workshop may be cancelled or rescheduled. Please keep this in mind when making travel reserverations.

Transportation and Accomodation

The CAE Montreal training centre is just past the runways of the Montreal—Pierre Elliot Trudeau International Airport (YUL, formerly known as the Dorval airport). You can also get to Montreal by Via Rail from Toronto, Ottawa, or Québec city, or by an all-day Amtrak train from New York City.

Local transportation is run by STM. Multiple bus routes pass by the venue, including evening service.

There are many hotels, in various price ranges, within 5km of the venue, some within walking distance or on the STM bus routes. Many of these will have airport shuttle services.

There is parking at CAE Montreal.

Online Participation

We are still working on the logistics for remote participation in the workshop. Our goal is to support both presentations and discussion from online participants. We will also have text-based chat (IRC) for discussion and for meeting minutes.
